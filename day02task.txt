Day 2 Observations

1. Number of Rows:
The dataset contains 24 rows of data.

2. Number of Columns:
There are 9 columns in the dataset.

3. Column Meaning:
- Order_ID: Unique ID for each sales transaction
- Employee_Name: Name of the employee who made the sale
- Department: Department (Electronics, Clothing, Furniture)
- Region: Sales location (North, South, East, West)
- Product_Category: Type of product sold (Mobiles, Laptops, etc.)
- Units_Sold: Number of units sold in that transaction
- Sales_Amount: Total sales value in currency
- Profit: Profit earned from the sale
- Date: Date of the transaction

4. One Surprising Thing:
I noticed that each employee appears multiple times with different dates and sales values,
which means the dataset records multiple transactions per employee. Also,
Electronics products seem to have higher sales and profit compared to other departments.
